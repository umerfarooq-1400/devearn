How to Get Your First Freelance Web Dev Client in Pakistan

June 2025 · 7 min read · Freelancing

Getting your first client is the hardest part of freelancing. No portfolio, no reviews, no proof you can deliver. Here is exactly how to land your first paying client in Pakistan — even with zero experience.

I started the same way. No clients, no reputation, just the ability to build a website. Here is the path that actually works.

Step 1 — Build Proof Before You Need It

Important

Nobody hires a developer with zero portfolio. Build 2-3 sample websites first — even fake businesses work. This becomes your proof.

Pick a niche — restaurants, wedding venues, clothing brands — and build one complete website for an imaginary business in that niche. Make it look real and professional. This is what you show clients.

Step 2 — Where to Find Clients

📱

Facebook Business Pages

Search local businesses without websites. DM them directly with a simple offer.

💼

Fiverr / Upwork

Create a gig, apply to small jobs first to build reviews quickly.

👥

Facebook Groups

Pakistani business owner groups often post "need a website" requests.

🗣️

Word of Mouth

Tell everyone you know. Relatives, friends, local shop owners — someone always needs a site.

Step 3 — The Outreach Message

Don't write long messages. Keep it short, direct, and focused on their problem — not your skills.

Hi [Name], I noticed [Business Name] doesn't have a website yet. I build clean, professional websites for local businesses — usually done within 3-5 days. Would you be interested in seeing a free sample design for your business?

This works because it's not asking for money upfront — it offers value first. Most businesses say yes to seeing a free sample.

Step 4 — The Free Sample Trick

01
They reply "yes, show me"

Now you have permission to build something for their actual business.

02
Build a quick homepage

Use their real business name, logo if available, and content. Takes 2-3 hours using your templates.

03
Send the live preview

Host it free on GitHub Pages and send the link. Seeing their own business online converts almost every time.

04
Ask for payment to finish

"This is just the homepage — I can complete the full site with [features] for Rs [price]."

Step 5 — Pricing for Your First Clients

Don't undercharge completely, but stay realistic for your first 3-5 projects:

Simple business website: Rs 8,000 - 15,000 E-commerce / booking site: Rs 20,000 - 40,000 With admin panel: Rs 35,000 - 60,000

After 5-6 projects with good reviews, raise your prices. Your first clients are about building proof, not maximizing income.

Key Takeaway

The free sample method works because it removes risk for the client. They see their own website before paying anything. Use this for your first 5 clients, then switch to charging upfront once you have reviews.
